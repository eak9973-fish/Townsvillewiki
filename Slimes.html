Slimes

Slimes

Slimes are amorphous lifeforms commonly found throughout Townsville. Though often mistaken for simple creatures, studies and eyewitness accounts suggest that slime abilities are heavily influenced by pigmentation, environment, and internal energy composition.

Biology

Slimes are composed of highly adhesive cells with a semi-fluid consistency comparable to dense gel. Despite lacking traditional organs, slimes demonstrate coordinated movement, environmental awareness, and limited problem-solving abilities. Their coloration is believed to directly correspond to their unique elemental or chemical control.

Documented Variants

Green Slimes

The most commonly encountered slime variant. Green slimes possess extremely adhesive cellular matter and emit trace amounts of gamma radiation. No harmful effects have been recorded. They are generally docile and often approach humans without hostility.

Red Slimes

Highly aggressive and territorial. Red slimes generate extreme internal heat, with body temperatures measured at approximately 200°F (93°C). Though dangerous at close range, their poor eyesight makes them easier to evade at a distance.

Orange Slimes

Among the friendliest known variants. Orange slimes naturally produce citric compounds and are frequently found near citrus farms and orchards. Due to their gentle nature, they are sometimes kept as pets by local farmers.

Purple Slimes

A timid and defensive species. Purple slimes can release clouds of toxic vapor when threatened, creating smoke screens to escape predators. They rarely initiate combat and are easily startled.

Blue Slimes

An aquatic variant capable of manipulating water. Blue slimes are often observed assisting drowning creatures by pushing them toward the surface. Coastal communities commonly refer to them as the "Lifeguards of the Sea."

Unconfirmed & Folklore Variants

Sightings of additional slime colors—including pink, white, and brown—have been reported by travelers and villagers, though none have been verified through direct study.

Black Slimes

Known in ancient records and myths as Voided Nulls, black slimes are said to erase matter, energy, and even memory itself. No confirmed encounters exist, and their existence remains the subject of speculation and folklore.
